Capstone Project: bloc Making For Everyone

Project Overview

As part of the final year in the Creative Technology and Design program, I completed a two-semester Senior Capstone project focused on collaborative problem-solving and innovation.

  • Timeline: September 2024 – May 2025
  • Team: Andrew Widner, Matt Bloomfield, Sophia Montie
  • Role: Design Technologist – UX design, testing, instructional design, front-end dev
  • Problem: CNC machining is inaccessible and intimidating for beginners
  • Solution: Gamified CNC interface, safety enclosure, beginner-friendly learning guide

Background & Context

We began by exploring the current landscape of STEM education and makerspaces. Our research revealed several key insights:

  • Lack of equitable access to STEM education in under-resourced areas
  • Barriers: limited funding, equipment, training
  • STEM felt intimidating to beginners
  • Belief: Anyone—regardless of age or background—should feel empowered to start making

Problem

  • Fabrication can often be intimidating for beginners due to necessary prior knowledge and difficult-to-use machines.
  • Most beginner machines on the market are either expensive or targeted towards younger audiences.
  • Tabletop CNC machines lack critical safety measures and in-depth instruction required to operate them.

Target Audience

  • Older children & middle schoolers (8-14)
  • Parents & educators
  • DIY makers & enthusiasts

Common Needs

Accessibility Often too expensive, complex, or unavailable for home or school use
STEM Gaps Existing tools lack practical, real-world applications and are too advanced for beginners, limiting their educational impact
Existing Curriculum Lecture heavy, with limited hands-on or creative expression
Learning Style STEM tools frequently overlook the needs of hands-on, exploratory learners who benefit from creative, open-ended activities
Home Engagement Parents need resources to reinforce school lessons effectively

Solution Oveview

We chose to focus on the CNC machine, a powerful, industry-standard tool, that remains less accessible than more common fabrication technologies like 3D printers or laser cutters. By centering our project around CNC machining, we aimed to bridge that gap and invite more people into the world of fabrication.

We modified an existing tabletop CNC machine, designed a safety enclosure, built a gamified joystick interface, and created an innovation guide to teach learners the fundamentals of CNC machining.

bloc Objectives & Goals

Make CNC Accessible

  • Safer: limit switches, stop button, compact tabletop enclosure, joystick interaction.
  • Affordability: built with cheaper materials (e.g. recycled PETG, plexiglass).

STEM Concept Exposure

  • Geometry and measurement: axes, angles, and measurements.
  • Design thinking and problem-solving: understand, ideate, prototype, test, and improve their designs.

Hands-on Skill Building

  • Fabrication techniques: parallel learning between tactile and technological tools.
  • Material handling: selecting, preparing, and working with different types of raw materials for fabrication.
  • Learning through: imitation → modification → innovation

My Role

As a Design Technologist, I worked at the intersection of UX design, technical implementation, and communication. My focus was on ensuring the system was intuitive, beginner-friendly, and sustainable for future users and builders. My key contributions included:

  • Implemented iterative user testing on console layout, learner motivation, and onboarding clarity
  • Tested usability of CNC machine and instruction guide to refine both physical and instructional interactions
  • Researched best practices via expert outreach to ensure innovation guide was accurate and approachable
  • Created a maintainable system for future builders and learners.
  • Led branding, photography, and documentation visuals
  • Developed the website and managed social media presence to spark interest in our project
